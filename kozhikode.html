KOZHIKODE

Kozhikode, also known as Calicut, is a coastal city located in the southern Indian state of Kerala. The city is known for its natural beauty, historic significance, and cultural heritage. One of the main attractions of Kozhikode is its beautiful beaches, which are known for their pristine waters and serene atmosphere. The Kappad Beach, which is located around 16 km from the city, is where the Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama first landed in India in 1498. The beach is popular among tourists and locals alike, and offers a peaceful and relaxing atmosphere. Kozhikode is also known for its historic significance and is home to a number of ancient temples, churches, and monuments. The Kozhikode Beach, which is situated in the heart of the city, is one of the main attractions in the city. The beach offers a panoramic view of the Arabian Sea and is a popular spot for evening strolls and sunset watching. The city is also famous for its traditional art forms, such as Theyyam and Thira. Theyyam is a popular ritualistic dance-drama that is unique to Kerala and is known for its elaborate costumes and makeup. Thira, on the other hand, is a traditional form of dance-drama that is performed during the temple festivals. Kozhikode is also known for its delicious cuisine, which includes both vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes. The city is famous for its Halwa, which is a sweet made of wheat, sugar, and ghee. The city is also known for its seafood, which includes a variety of fish, prawns, and crabs. In terms of transportation, Kozhikode is well-connected to other parts of Kerala and India. The city has a major railway station and is also connected by road and air. The Calicut International Airport is located around 28 km from the city and offers regular flights to major cities in India and abroad. Overall, Kozhikode is a beautiful city that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, historic significance, and cultural heritage. Whether you are interested in exploring the beaches, ancient temples, or traditional art forms, Kozhikode has something for everyone.

PLACES TO VISIT

1.Kozhikode Beach

Situated on the Malabar coast and endowed with natural beauty, Kozhikode beach should be a must in the itinerary of your visit to the city. This beach is renowned for the sunsets that tourists throng here to enjoy. Though the water is muddy and not suitable for swimming, still the stunning view of the Arabian Sea that the lighthouse offers more than compensates for the shortcoming. Payannakal, Putiyappa, Elathur, Beypore, all these beaches are part of the long stretch of Kozhikode beach. This beach lies on the western side of the city and is easily accessible through the four roads in the city.

2.Kakkadampoyil Waterfall

Kakkadampoyil is a sleepy village in Koodaranhi panchayat in Kozhikode. This village has sprung up as an idyllic tourist spot because of the scenic beauty and the famous waterfall of the same name. The waterfall that lies on the Kuruvan River has been drawing visitors with the abundance of flora and fauna, who love a break from city life. The misty mountains and the hills surrounding it has given this place another name “Mini Gavi”, by drawing a parallel with the Gavi forests. One of the top places to visit in Kozhikode, the waterfall and Pazhassi caves are the main tourist attractions here. The “Mini Gavi” forest is inhabited with elephants, tigers, birds, and butterflies and is the chief attraction for nature lovers and wildlife photographers. This is the perfect spot for trekking.
