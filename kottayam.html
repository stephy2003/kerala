KOTTAYAM

Kottayam is a district located in the southern Indian state of Kerala. It is known for its scenic beauty, cultural heritage, and educational institutions. The district is situated on the banks of the Meenachil River and is surrounded by the Western Ghats on one side and the Vembanad Lake on the other. One of the main attractions of Kottayam is its scenic beauty, which is characterized by lush green landscapes, rolling hills, and serene backwaters. The Vembanad Lake, which is one of the largest freshwater lakes in Asia, is a popular tourist destination and offers a peaceful and serene atmosphere. The Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, which is located on the banks of the lake, is a paradise for birdwatchers and nature lovers. Kottayam is also known for its cultural heritage and is home to a number of ancient temples, churches, and monuments. The St. Mary's Church in Kottayam is one of the oldest churches in Kerala and is known for its beautiful architecture and religious significance. The Thirunakkara Mahadeva Temple, which is dedicated to Lord Shiva, is another popular attraction in the city. The district is also famous for its educational institutions and is considered as the educational hub of Kerala. The Mahatma Gandhi University, which is located in Kottayam, is one of the leading universities in the state and offers courses in a wide range of disciplines. Kottayam is also known for its delicious cuisine, which includes both vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes. Some of the popular dishes in the district include Appam, Puttu, and Meen Curry. In terms of transportation, Kottayam is well-connected to other parts of Kerala and India. The city has a major railway station and is also connected by road and air. The nearest airport is the Cochin International Airport, which is located around 90 km from the city. Overall, Kottayam is a beautiful district that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and educational institutions. Whether you are interested in exploring the backwaters, ancient temples, or educational institutions, Kottayam has something for everyone.

PLACES TO VISIT

1.Marmalade Waterfalls

The Marmalade Waterfalls, which are only a few miles from Erattupetta and exactly 8 kilometres from Jeekoy, are the ideal destination for adventure. The mesmerizing beauty of the waterfalls gives the difficult and exhausting trek there rewarding. The view of the sparkling waters falling down clean and bright rocks at the Marmala Waterfalls is truly mesmerizing, best seasons to visit the waterfalls are spring and summer when the water is warm enough for a bath. Visitors should stay away during the rainy season since trekking and mountaineering may be difficult due to lightning and thunderstorms. Marmala Road in Vellani Erattupetta, Kerala, is the destination. Opening times are every week from 6 am to 6 pm.

2.Taj Juma Masjid

On the banks of the Meenachil river sits this 1,000-Year-old mosque, which claims to be the oldest mosque in Kerala. The mosque is renowned for its countless examples of historical and cultural elegance.It has become a popular destination for visitors and relaxation lovers due to the beauty of its architectural design, beautiful woodwork, and lush green surroundings. The mosque's natural beauty is extremely outstanding and is similar to that of a palace. The traditional Indo-Russian influence of the fresco painting at the mosque will also be available for tourists to enjoy. Its serene and spiritual atmosphere makes for a wonderful vacation. Tourist Bangalow Road, across from Joy Alukkas, is where you'll find Eerayii Kadavu in Kottayam, Kerala. Opening times are Every day of the week, from 6 am until 8.
