WAYANAD

Wayanad is a district located in the northern part of Kerala, a southern state of India. The district is known for its natural beauty, rich biodiversity, and traditional tribal culture. One of the main attractions of Wayanad is its lush green forests and scenic hills. The district is home to several wildlife sanctuaries and national parks, including the Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary, which is home to a variety of flora and fauna. The district is also known for its waterfalls, such as the Soochipara Waterfalls and the Meenmutty Waterfalls, which attract tourists from all over the world. Wayanad is also known for its traditional tribal culture, with several tribal communities residing in the district. The district is home to several tribal museums and cultural centers, where tourists can learn more about the traditional way of life and art forms of the tribal communities. The district is also famous for its delicious cuisine, which includes both vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes. The district is known for its traditional rice-based dishes, such as the Malabar Biriyani and the Puttu-Kadala. The district is also known for its traditional sweets, such as the Kalathappam and the Unniappam. In terms of transportation, Wayanad is well-connected to other parts of Kerala and India. The district has a major railway station and is also connected by road and air. The nearest airport is the Calicut International Airport, which is located around 100 km from the district. Overall, Wayanad is a beautiful district that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, traditional tribal culture, and delicious cuisine. Whether you are interested in exploring the wildlife sanctuaries, learning about the traditional tribal culture, or trying out the traditional dishes, Wayanad has something for everyone.

PLACES TO VISIT

1. Meenmutty Waterfalls

For all those who love to know no boundaries for nature, Meenmutty Waterfalls can be the right place to visit. It is amongst the best waterfalls in Kerala that offers a delightful experience to people of all age groups. Surrounded by verdant green forests, this three-tiered waterfall falls from a height of 1000 feet and provides its visitors with a fantastic view that is a sight to behold. To reach the waterfalls, you need to trek through the thick jungles for nearly 2 km to enjoy a kind of adventure that is an experience in itself. You can also engage in photography to capture some excellent shots of Mother Nature. Please avoid visiting it during the rainy season as the waterfall becomes extremely dangerous due to the heavy flow of water. Entry Fee: INR 30 per person for adults, INR 15 per person for children, INR 60 per person for foreigners, and INR 75 for a still camera Timings: Anytime between 9:00 AM and 5:30 PM Exploration Time: 2-3 hours Best Time to Visit: October to May

2. Chembra Peak

The highest mountain peak in the Wayanad hill range, the Chembra Peak invites nature admirers to adore its majestic beauty along with enjoying a spectacular view of the Wayanad, Nilgiri, Malappuram, and Kozhikode districts from a height of 2100 meters above sea level. On the way, you will come across several small waterfalls, abundant lush greenery, a diverse range of flora and fauna, and a heart-shaped lake that is believed to never dry up, no matter what the season is. Coming to the trekking charges, it costs INR 750 for a group of 10 Indian nationals and INR 1500 for a group of 5 foreign nationals.
