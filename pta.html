PATHANAMTHITTA

Pathanamthitta is a district located in the southern Indian state of Kerala. The district is known for its natural beauty, religious significance, and cultural heritage. One of the main attractions of Pathanamthitta is its scenic beauty, which is characterized by lush green landscapes, rolling hills, and serene backwaters. The district is home to the Periyar National Park, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its unique biodiversity. The park is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including elephants, tigers, and the Nilgiri langur. Pathanamthitta is also known for its religious significance and is home to a number of ancient temples, churches, and mosques. The Sabarimala Temple, which is located in the district, is one of the most famous pilgrimage sites in India and attracts millions of devotees every year. The temple is dedicated to Lord Ayyappa and is known for its strict rules and regulations for devotees. The district is also famous for its traditional art forms, such as Kathakali and Mohiniyattam. Kathakali is a classical dance-drama that is known for its elaborate costumes and makeup, while Mohiniyattam is a classical dance form that is unique to Kerala. Pathanamthitta is also known for its delicious cuisine, which includes both vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes. The district is famous for its traditional fish curry, which is made with coconut milk and a blend of spices. The district is also known for its traditional sweets, such as the Kerala-style payasam and the ada. In terms of transportation, Pathanamthitta is well-connected to other parts of Kerala and India. The district has a major railway station and is also connected by road and air. The nearest airport is the Trivandrum International Airport, which is located around 110 km from the district. Overall, Pathanamthitta is a beautiful district that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, religious significance, and cultural heritage. Whether you are interested in exploring the national park, ancient temples, or traditional art forms, Pathanamthitta has something for everyone.

PLACES TO VISIT

1.Gavi

At a distance of 14 km from Pathanamthitta, Gavi is located. In addition to cardamom plantations, it is surrounded by spectacular waterfalls and tea plantations. Among the activities you can enjoy in Gavi are trekking, wildlife watching, and outdoor activities.

2.Konni Forest Reserve

The Konni Forest Reserve is a popular tourist destination located in Pathanamthitta. The reserve is home to a variety of wildlife, including elephants, tigers, and leopards. Konni is also home to the largest population of Asiatic elephants in Kerala. To reach Konni, take a bus or taxi from Pathanamthitta city centre. With lush pastures and hills along the Achankovil river, Konni is an attractive town 11 km from Pathanamthitta.
