MALAPPURAM

Malappuram is a district located in the northern part of the Indian state of Kerala. The district is known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic beauty, and educational institutions. Malappuram is situated on the banks of the Bharathapuzha River and is surrounded by the Western Ghats on one side and the Arabian Sea on the other. One of the main attractions of Malappuram is its natural beauty, which is characterized by lush green landscapes, rolling hills, and serene backwaters. The Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary, which is located in the district, is a popular destination for birdwatchers and nature lovers. The sanctuary is home to a wide variety of migratory and resident birds, including terns, gulls, sandpipers, and plovers. Malappuram is also known for its rich cultural heritage and is home to a number of ancient temples, mosques, and monuments. The Thirunavaya Temple, which is dedicated to Lord Vishnu, is one of the oldest temples in the district and is known for its beautiful architecture and religious significance. The Jama Masjid in Malappuram is one of the largest mosques in Kerala and is known for its unique blend of Indian and Islamic architecture. The district is also famous for its educational institutions and is considered as one of the major educational hubs in Kerala. The Calicut University, which is located in Malappuram, is one of the leading universities in the state and offers courses in a wide range of disciplines. Malappuram is also known for its delicious cuisine, which includes both vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes. The district is famous for its biryani, which is a spicy rice dish that is made with chicken or mutton. The district is also known for its traditional sweets, such as halwa and laddoo. In terms of transportation, Malappuram is well-connected to other parts of Kerala and India. The district has a major railway station and is also connected by road and air. The nearest airport is the Calicut International Airport, which is located around 28 km from the district. Overall, Malappuram is a beautiful district that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and educational institutions. Whether you are interested in exploring the backwaters, ancient temples, or educational institutions, Malappuram has something for everyone.

PLACES TO VISIT

1.Bharathapuzha River

Visit this charming river, which originates from the Anamalai Hills, to relax after a stressful day at work. On its shores, you may relax for hours while enjoying the fresh breezes and the beauty of the natural surroundings. You may also visit all the respected temples on its shore if you want to learn more. Additionally, if you time your trip to connect with the Karkidaka Vavu festival, you'll observe people visiting the river to show respect to their dead relatives.

2.Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary

This sanctuary is a fantastic choice among Malappuram's day-trip destinations. At this sanctuary, you will interact with various recognized and undiscovered wildlife. But there's more. You will also enjoy the forest's greenery. You can choose between taking a boat trip or an exhilarating journey to explore the region fully. You may anticipate seeing many crabs, fish, and mussels while sailing. Don't forget to bring your camera; you'll have many chances to take beautiful wildlife photos. To access this sanctuary, all you need to do is pay a 25-rupee entrance charge. Additionally, the opening times are 8 am to 6 pm.
