THRISSUR

Thrissur, also known as Trichur, is a district located in the southern Indian state of Kerala. The district is known for its rich cultural heritage, historical significance, and traditional art forms. One of the main attractions of Thrissur is its cultural heritage, which is characterized by a rich history and a vibrant tradition of art and literature. The district is home to a number of ancient temples, churches, and monuments, including the famous Vadakkunnathan Temple, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is known for its beautiful architecture and cultural significance. Thrissur is also known for its traditional art forms, such as Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, and Theyyam. Kathakali is a classical dance-drama that is known for its elaborate costumes and makeup, while Mohiniyattam is a classical dance form that is unique to Kerala. Theyyam is a ritualistic art form that is performed in various parts of Kerala and is known for its vibrant colors and dramatic music. The district is also famous for its delicious cuisine, which includes both vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes. The district is famous for its traditional fish curry, which is made with coconut milk and a blend of spices. The district is also known for its traditional sweets, such as the Kerala-style payasam and the elaneer pudding. In terms of transportation, Thrissur is well-connected to other parts of Kerala and India. The district has a major railway station and is also connected by road and air. The nearest airport is the Cochin International Airport, which is located around 55 km from the district. Overall, Thrissur is a beautiful district that offers a unique blend of cultural heritage, traditional art forms, and delicious cuisine. Whether you are interested in exploring the ancient temples, witnessing the vibrant art forms, or trying out the traditional dishes, Thrissur has something for everyone.

PLACES TO VISIT

1.Athirappilly Waterfalls

On the outskirts of the city at a distance of some 60 km, the Athirappilly waterfalls mark one of the best places to visit in Thrissur. Standing 80 ft tall and about 330 ft wide, these falls are the largest in the state of Kerala. The majesty of this place can be understood by the fact that the falls are located on the Chalakudy River, that originates from the highest mountain in India outside of the Himalayas. A perfect place for a group outing, these falls are sometimes called the Niagra of India

2.Vadakkunnathan Temple

An ancient temple of Kerala, the Vadakkunnathan Temple is also the oldest temple of the state dedicated to Lord Shiva. Following the architectural style of Kerala, the temple has monumental towers surrounding it. Talking about the inside of the temple, there are several mural paintings that depict various episodes from the Hindu Epic, Mahabharata. Thekkinkadu Maidan, which surrounds the temple from all sides is where the local festival of Thrissur Pooram is organized every year, granting this place an even more of spiritual importance.
