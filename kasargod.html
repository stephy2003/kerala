KASARAGOD

Kasaragod is a district located in the northernmost part of the Indian state of Kerala. It is known for its serene beaches, backwaters, and lush green landscapes. The district shares its borders with the state of Karnataka and is situated on the coast of the Arabian Sea. One of the main attractions of Kasaragod is its beautiful beaches. Some of the popular beaches in the district include Bekal Beach, Kappil Beach, and Cheruvathur Beach. These beaches offer a tranquil and peaceful atmosphere, making them perfect for relaxation and unwinding. Kasaragod is also known for its scenic backwaters, which offer a unique and serene experience. The Chandragiri and Valiyaparamba backwaters are popular among tourists and are perfect for boating, fishing, and birdwatching. The district is also home to a number of historic sites and monuments. The Bekal Fort, which is located on a hilltop overlooking the sea, is one of the main attractions in Kasaragod. The fort, which was built in the 17th century, is a fine example of ancient Indian architecture and provides a stunning view of the sea. Kasaragod is also known for its traditional art forms, such as Yakshagana and Theyyam. Yakshagana is a folk dance-drama that originated in Karnataka and is popular in the Kasaragod region. Theyyam is a unique form of worship that involves elaborate costumes, music, and dance. In terms of transportation, Kasaragod is well-connected by road, rail, and air. The nearest airport is the Mangalore International Airport, which is located around 60 km from the district. The district is also connected by the Konkan Railway and National Highway 66. Overall, Kasaragod is a beautiful district that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, historic sites, and cultural traditions. Whether you are interested in exploring the beaches, backwaters, or traditional art forms, Kasaragod has something for everyone.

PLACES TO VISIT

1.Bekal Fort

Bekal Fort is a historical structure built in the 17th century and has itself become part of an identity of the district. It is situated in Pallikara village of Kasaragod on a headland that spans up to 35 km.It is believed that the fort was originally constructed by rulers of the ancient Kadampa dynasty and the power changed hands over the years to Kolathiri Rajas, Hyder Ali, Tipu Sultan and finally to the British East India Company. The lofty, circular laterite structure of the fort that stands at a height of 130 feet is shaped like a giant keyhole. The walls around rise to 12 metres in height. The highlights include tall observation towers which presumably would have been occupied by gigantic cannons until a few centuries ago, a water tank with its flight of steps and a tunnel that opens towards the south. The architectural beauty of the fort also met the defence requirements of various rulers,which was the main reason for the fortâ€™s existence. There are also some pilgrim attractions nearby like the Lord Hanuman temple and an old Mosque which is believed to be built by Tippu Sultan.The Bekal beach is only a kilometre away from the fort which offers an enticing view of the imposing fort. It has now become a favoured destination for weddings, ad shoots and filmmakers for its picturesque location. This is the largest and the best-preserved fort in the district and is now maintained by the Archaeological Department of Government of India.

2.Chandragiri Fort

Chandragiri Fort is another historical landmark in the district situated in the south-east of Kasaragod town. The Chandragiri River that flows nearby once bordered the kingdoms of Kolathunadu and Tulunadu. The origin of the fort goes back to the times of Keladi Nayakas who took control of the region as the Vijayanagara Empire fell. Shivappa Nayak secured his empire by renovating and constructing many forts around the area one of which is Chandragiri which was built in the 17th century. The remnants of the fort which can be seen on a small hill overlook the estuary where the Payaswini River joins the Arabian Sea. The old laterite walls around the fort still bear marks of cannonball attacks. The ancient Kizhur Sastha temple and a mosque are situated near the fort. The fort is now maintained by the Archaeology Department of India. Boat services are available here that will take you to nearby islands and palm groves. Speed boat rides,houseboat cruises, island camping and wildlife tours in the hinterlands can also be arranged.
