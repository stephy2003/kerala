ERNAKULAM

Ernakulam is a city located in the state of Kerala, India. It is often referred to as the commercial capital of Kerala due to its thriving economy and bustling business district. The city is situated on the coast of the Arabian Sea and is home to a population of over 2.5 million people. One of the main attractions of Ernakulam is its rich cultural heritage. The city is home to a number of historical sites, including the Hill Palace Museum, which was the former residence of the royal family of Kochi. The museum contains a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the rich history and culture of Kerala. Another popular attraction in Ernakulam is the famous Bolgatty Palace, which was built in the 18th century by the Dutch. The palace is now a luxury hotel and is known for its beautiful gardens and stunning views of the backwaters. Ernakulam is also known for its stunning natural beauty. The city is surrounded by lush green hills and is home to a number of parks and gardens, including the famous Marine Drive, which is a popular spot for evening walks and sunset views. The food in Ernakulam is also something that draws visitors from all over the world. The city is famous for its traditional Kerala cuisine, which includes dishes such as appam, dosa, and fish curry. There are a number of restaurants and street food vendors in Ernakulam that offer delicious and authentic Kerala food. In terms of transportation, Ernakulam is well-connected to other parts of Kerala and India. The city has a major railway station and is also connected by road and air. The Cochin International Airport is located in the neighboring city of Kochi and offers flights to both domestic and international destinations. Overall, Ernakulam is a vibrant and dynamic city that offers a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you are interested in exploring the rich history and culture of Kerala, enjoying the stunning natural scenery, or indulging in the delicious local cuisine, Ernakulam has something for everyone.

PLACES TO VISIT

1.The Museum of Mattancherry

A stroll through the lanes of Fort Kochi will definitely attract you to the rustic and old-fashioned neighbourhood of Mattancherry. It is said that back in the days, this cheri (street), was lined with mutton butchers, and so it ended up being called “Muttoncherry” which over the years evolved into “Mattancherry”. Today the place exhibits centuries-old architectures and various social and cultural imprints left behind by foreigners. Declared as a heritage site by the government, Mattancherry has been an inspiration to historians, film-makers, travellers and artistes alike. Amongst all the Ernakulam tourist places, this museum is one of the most prominent attractions.

2.Folklore Museum

Folklore Museum is one of the popular tourist spots in the city. It is a three storied complex that showcases cultural exhibits and tribal artefacts. The Museum design was greatly inspired by the Malabar architecture. The museum is opened to the public from 9: 30 Am to 7 PM on a regular basis.
